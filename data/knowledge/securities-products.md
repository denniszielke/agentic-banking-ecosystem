# 1. Securities & Depot Products

> A depot (securities/custody account) holds a customer's investments — funds,
> ETFs and shares. Its balance is the current **market value** of the portfolio,
> not a cash balance, and it does not pay a deposit interest rate.

## 1.1. Securities Depot

### 1.1.1. Product Description
**1.1.1.1** Securities Depot is a self-directed custody account for customers who
want to buy and hold funds, ETFs and shares themselves. The balance reflects the
current market value of the held positions.

### 1.1.2. Conditions

| Ref | Attribute | Value |
|-----|---------|-----|
| 1.1.2.1 | Deposit Interest Rate | Not applicable (market-value account) |
| 1.1.2.2 | Custody Fee | Free |
| 1.1.2.3 | Order Fee (per trade) | €4.90 flat |
| 1.1.2.4 | Savings Plan Fee | Free |
| 1.1.2.5 | Minimum Investment | €0 |
| 1.1.2.6 | Advice | Execution-only (no advice) |
| 1.1.2.7 | Online Banking | Included |
| 1.1.2.8 | Mobile Banking | Included |
| 1.1.2.9 | Requires Current Account | Yes |
| 1.1.2.10 | Risk Level | Medium–High (market risk) |

### 1.1.3. Suitable For
- 1.1.3.1 Self-directed investors
- 1.1.3.2 Long-term wealth building
- 1.1.3.3 Customers comfortable with market risk

## 1.2. Wealth Depot

### 1.2.1. Product Description
**1.2.1.1** Wealth Depot is a managed custody account: the portfolio is
constructed and rebalanced by the bank's investment team against the customer's
risk profile. The balance reflects the current market value of the managed
portfolio.

### 1.2.2. Conditions

### 1.2.3. Suitable For
- 1.2.3.1 Customers who prefer a hands-off, managed portfolio
- 1.2.3.2 Larger investable sums (€25,000+)
- 1.2.3.3 Long-term wealth management

# 3. Agent Guidance

## 3.1. Customer With Idle Cash Seeking Higher Returns

**3.1.1** A depot is **not** a substitute for a savings account and does not pay a
guaranteed interest rate — its value moves with the market.

### 3.1.2. Reasoning
- 3.1.2.1 Depots carry market risk; savings accounts do not.
- 3.1.2.2 For guaranteed returns on idle cash, prefer a savings product.
- 3.1.2.3 A depot suits money the customer can invest for the long term.

### 3.1.3. Agent Statement
> **3.1.3.1** "A depot lets you invest for the long term, but its value can rise
> and fall. For money you may need soon, a savings account gives a guaranteed
> rate; a depot is for longer-term investing where you accept market risk."

---

## 3.2. Self-Directed vs Managed

**3.2.1** **Recommended Products:** Securities Depot or Wealth Depot

### 3.2.2. Decision Guidance

#### 3.2.2.1. Choose **Securities Depot** if
- 3.2.2.1.1 The customer wants to pick investments themselves
- 3.2.2.1.2 Fee sensitivity is high (free custody, low order fee)

#### 3.2.2.2. Choose **Wealth Depot** if
- 3.2.2.2.1 The customer prefers a professionally managed portfolio
- 3.2.2.2.2 The investable sum is at least €25,000

### 3.2.3. Agent Statement
> **3.2.3.1** "If you'd like to manage investments yourself, Securities Depot has
> free custody and a low per-trade fee. If you'd rather it was managed for you,
> Wealth Depot handles construction and rebalancing for an annual fee."

# 5. Compliance Rules

## 5.1. The agent may

- 5.1.1 Explain depot features, fees and risk levels
- 5.1.2 Compare the available depot products
- 5.1.3 Explain that a depot's value carries market risk

### 5.1.4. The agent must not

- 5.1.4.1 Provide personalised investment advice
- 5.1.4.2 Recommend specific securities, funds or ETFs
- 5.1.4.3 Guarantee investment returns
- 5.1.4.4 Open or fund a depot without a suitability check and human approval

**5.1.4.5** Any move into investment advice or suitability assessment must be
referred to a qualified financial advisor.
